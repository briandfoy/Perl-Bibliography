Get a clear roadmap for improving your skills with Intermediate Perl,
and gain working knowledge of Perl's objects, references, and
modules—ingredients that make the language so versatile and effective.
Written by the authors of the bestselling Llama book, Learning Perl,
and updated for Perl 5.14, this book offers a gentle but thorough
introduction to intermediate programming in Perl.

Each chapter is small enough to be read in just an hour or two, ending
with a series of exercises to help you practice what you've learned.
If you’re familiar with the material in Learning Perl and have the
ambition learn more, this book will take you much further into Perl's
capabilities.

Topics include:

Packages and namespaces
References and scoping, including regular expression references
Manipulating complex data structures
Object-oriented programming
Writing and using modules
Testing Perl code
Contributing to CPAN
